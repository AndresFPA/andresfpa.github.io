About Me

I am a Postdoctoral Researcher at the Research Unit for Quantitative Psychology and Individual Differences at KU Leuven. My academic research focuses on developing novel statistical methodologies for social and behavioral sciences.

Specializing in Mixture Modeling and Structural Equation Modeling (SEM), my research has focused on addressing how to (efficiently) compare structural relations across numerous groups using clustering techniques (more about this in my selected publications). My career path has also allowed me to interact with a diverse range of statistical methods, including predictive modeling, machine learning, large language models, simulation studies, among others.
